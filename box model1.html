ABOUT Pizza

Pizza is an Italian, specifically Neapolitan, dish typically consisting of a flat base of leavened wheat-based dough topped with tomato, cheese, and other ingredients, baked at a high temperature, traditionally in a wood-fired oven.

Here is a step-by-step guide to making homemade pizza from scratch, including recipes for both the dough and sauce. Part 1: Make the pizza dough This recipe uses warm water to "bloom" the yeast, which activates it for a proper rise. Ingredients ¾ cup warm water (100–110°F) 1 packet active dry yeast (2¼ tsp) 1 tbsp granulated sugar, divided 2 cups all-purpose flour 1 tbsp olive oil, plus more for brushing ¾ tsp salt Instructions Activate the yeast. Combine the yeast mixture with other ingredients and knead until smooth. Let the dough rise until it doubles in size. Punch down and turn the dough onto a floured surface. For more detailed instructions, see Preppy Kitchen preppykitchen.com. Part 2: Make the pizza sauce This simple recipe makes a quick, tangy sauce. Ingredients 6 oz tomato paste 15 oz tomato sauce 1–2 tbsp dried oregano, to taste 2 tbsp Italian seasoning ½ tsp garlic powder ½ tsp onion powder ½ tbsp garlic salt ¼ tsp black pepper 1 tsp sugar Instructions Mix tomato paste and tomato sauce. Stir in the seasonings. Adjust seasonings to taste. Part 3: Assemble and bake your pizza Preheating your oven with a pizza stone is recommended for a crispy crust. Instructions Preheat your oven with a pizza stone or baking sheet to its highest temperature (450–550°F) for at least 30 minutes. Stretch or roll the dough on a floured surface. Place the dough on parchment paper, spread sauce, and add toppings. Bake for 10–12 minutes until done. Let the pizza cool slightly before serving.
